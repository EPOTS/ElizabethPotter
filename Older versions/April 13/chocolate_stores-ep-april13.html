NYC Chocolatiers

Li-Lac Chocolates

40 8th Ave., New York, NY (212) 924-2280

Li-Lac Chocolates is Manhattan's oldest chocolate house -a New York tradition since 1923. Making small batches for exceptional quality ingredients. With more than 140 items, with one of the largest selections of fresh gourmet chocolate in America. Every delicious item is made by hand, locally in New York City and guaranteed for freshness. Li-Lac Chocolates is one of the few old-school chocolate companies to survive into the modern era.

Serendipity 3

225 East 60th St., New York, NY (212) 838-3531

Serendipity

Serendipity was established in 1954, it has been popular since the very beginning. Where famous people such as Marilyn Monroe frequently patronized this location and so did First Lady Jackie O. In 2004, Serendipity celebrated its 50th anniversary by introducing the record-breaking Golden Opulence Sundae, listed in the Guiness Book of World Records as the most expensive dessert, at $1000.

Mast Brothers

111 N. 3rd St., Brooklyn, NY (718) 388-2625

Mast Brothers Chocolate Factory

Once upon a time, 2 brothers began making chocolate-literally from bean to bar- in their Brooklyn, NY, apartment, perfecting each piece with artisanal touch and wrapping them with butcher paper. Today, Mast Brothers has set up a Brooklyn Chocolate Factory. Where they offer tours of the factory daily. As the tour comes to a close, guests enjoy 5 chocolates so they can discover how different beans yield different but mind-bogglingly delicious flavors.
